The life of "A-Rod"

Biography

A-Rod was born on July 27, 1975, in New York City. He is the youngest of three siblings. A-Rod was born into a baseball family. His dad was a professional catcher in the Dominican Republic. At age 4, Rodriguez moved with his family to the Dominican Republic, which is where he first started picking up a bat and ball. When Rodriguez reached fifth-grade, he relocated to Miami. Once they moved to Miami, Alex's parents parted ways, leaving his mother to take care of him.

Life in Miami

In high school, everyone knew who Alex Rodriguez was. He was a talented football player, but on the baseball field he was a different animal. In his junior year he had a batting avgerage of .477 and stole 42 bases in just 35 games. In his senior year, Rodriguez made the decision to devote his time to strictly baseball. After his junior year, he made the decision to commit to the University of Miami. Although his plan was to attent UM, after Rodriguez's senior year, he was selected No.1 overall in the major league baseballs 1993 draft by the Seattle Mariners. He decided to go pro signing a three year, $1.3 million dollar contract.

Major League Dreams

Rodriguez's rise through the Seattle Mariners system was astonishing. He made his debut in the Major league in 1994, a few weeks before his 19th birthday. Just to put into perspective most baseball players dont get called up to the major leagues until the age of 23+. His first two years in the MLB were average, but by 1996, A-rod, was a star. That year he batted .358, smashed 36 home runs and recorded 123 runs batted in to finish second in the MVP race. Over the next decade, Rodriguez proved to be one of baseball greatest stars in the game, puttin up numbers to put him on track to be one of baseball's greatest.
